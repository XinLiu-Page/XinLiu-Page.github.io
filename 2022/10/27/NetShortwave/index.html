The influence of dusts on radiation and temperature over the eastern Asia with a regional climate model

In order to investigate the climate effects of dusts, a regional climate model (RegCM 4.6) with the dust scheme was used to simulate the direct radiative forcing and air temperature response at 2 m near surface of dusts over the eastern Asia. Two sets of experiments were conducted, one with and one without dust aerosols. The experiment covered the main dust occurrence months from March to May for 8 years (2011−2018), and the simulation results were evaluated against ground station, reanalysis and satellite data.

Evaluation of net shortwave radiation over China with a regional climate model

The regional climate model RegCM version 4.6, developed by the European Centre for Medium-Range Weather Forecasts Reanalysis, was used to simulate the radiation budget over China. Clouds and the Earth’s Radiant Energy System (CERES) satellite data were utilized to evaluate the simulation results based on 4 radiative components: net shortwave (NSW) radiation at the surface of the earth and top of the atmosphere (TOA) under all-sky and clear-sky conditions.

Evaluation of simulation results from the two cumulus parameterization schemes in RegCM4.6 in East Asia

The regional climate model is widely used to predict the impact of changes in various factors on climate, track historical climate trends, and project future climate variations, but the selection of cumulus convective parameterization scheme is crucial for the accuracy of simulation results. There were three commonly used parameterization schemes named Grell, Emanuel and Kuo, and different schemes have different performance over different regions. The previous studies showed that the Emanuel scheme performs relatively good in the simulation of temperature and precipitation in East Asia. However, the user’s guide of RegCM4.6 points out that the Emanuel scheme tends to produce excessive precipitation over lands, especially in some intense individual precipitation events. On the contrary, the Grell scheme tends to produce weak precipitation over tropical oceans. Therefore, the new version RegCM4.6 has added the Mix cumulus convective parameterization scheme, which means the Emanuel scheme is used over oceans and the Grell scheme over land, in order to compensate for the deficiencies of a single scheme. The previous validation researches mainly focused on temperature and precipitation, and there was very little study about the Mix scheme. In this paper, the MODIS product was used as a reference to evaluate the simulation results of cloud fraction (CF), ice water path (IWP) and liquid water path (LWP) in East Asia from the Emanuel and Mix schemes in RegCM4.6 at various time scale. Some statistic parameters were calculated, such as the correlation coefficient (r), mean absolute error (MAE), mean bias error (MBE) and root mean square error (RMSE).

Seasonal variation in particle contribution and aerosol types in Shanghai based on satellite data from MODIS and CALIOP

The spatio-seasonal variation in aerosol optical properties and aerosol types were studied over a 10-year period (2006–2015) in Shanghai, China, using satellite data from Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer (MODIS) and Cloud-Aerosol Lidar with Orthogonal Polarization (CALIOP).

Preliminary study on impact of aerosols on surface solar radiation in Xinjiang

In order to further understand the impact of atmospheric aerosols on the climate and environment, Aerosol optical depth(AOD) derived from Aqua MODIS C006 dataset, surface short-wave radiation from CERES SSF Aqua MODIS Edition 4 A dataset, solar radiation data from ground sites are used to study the annual variation of AOD and surface solar radiation in 2017. And then taking the Kashi site as an example, a typical southern Xinjiang region rich in dust and human activities, combined with the AccuRT radiative transfer model, the impact of AOD on the surface shortwave radiation was quantified in clear-sky.
